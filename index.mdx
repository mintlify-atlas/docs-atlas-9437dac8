mount-scripts gives you a simple, repeatable way to manage CIFS/SMB network share mounts on Linux. Define your shares once in a `.env` file, then mount or unmount them with a single command. For shares behind a Cloudflare Access policy, built-in tunnel management handles authentication and forwarding automatically.

## How it works

mount-scripts reads your share and tunnel definitions from a `.env` file in the same directory as the scripts. Each mount is identified by a short name — you use that name when mounting, unmounting, or managing tunnels.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install dependencies">
    Install `cifs-utils` (required for all mounts) and optionally `cloudflared` for Cloudflare Tunnel support.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your mounts">
    Copy `.env.example` to `.env` and define your `MOUNT_*` and `TUNNEL_*` entries.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make scripts executable">
    Run `chmod +x mount-network umount-network start-cf-tunnel stop-cf-tunnel` once.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mount a share">
    Run `sudo ./mount-network <name>` to mount any configured share by name.
  </Step>
</Steps>
